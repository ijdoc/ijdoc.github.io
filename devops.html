DevOps

Fail fast, fail often.

Computer vector created by upklyak - www.freepik.com

I think DevOps makes development more human. not only because it amplifies the power of Engineering teams, but also because it implicitly recognizes failure as an essential component of the development process.

DevOps Gives You Wings.

As CTO of tecla (2010 - 2018) I implemented the continuous integration (CI) and continuous development (CD) pipelines that enabled a handful of Engineers (3 on average) to test, maintain and continuously deploy two mobile (Android and iOS) apps, as well as orchestrate OTA firmware releases for our flagship product.

Without these automated DevOps pipelines, it would have been impossible to run such an aggressively lean operation.

DevOps Gives You Wings.

As Senior DevOps Engineer at Peraso, I used iPXE, Python scripts, and a Flask/Teamcity service backend, to automate monitoring and recovery of testing setups for the company's WiGig chipsets, thus reducing failure downtime from days to minutes.

This performance improvement made setup failure inconsequential to core operations, allowing the DevOps team to focus on further optimizations.
